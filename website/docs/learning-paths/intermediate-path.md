## Intermediate Learning Path: Applied Humanoid Robotics

Welcome to the intermediate learning path! This path is designed for software developers, engineers, or researchers who have some experience with robotics or programming. The curriculum focuses on practical implementation of humanoid robotics concepts and systems.

### Prerequisites
- Programming experience (Python/C++)
- Basic understanding of linear algebra and calculus
- Some exposure to robotics concepts (preferred but not required)
- Familiarity with command-line tools

### Expected Duration
12-16 weeks (4-5 hours per week)

### Learning Outcomes
By completing this path, you will:
- Develop advanced robot applications using ROS 2
- Implement perception and control systems for humanoid robots
- Design complex robot behaviors and navigation systems
- Integrate multiple AI systems for comprehensive robot capabilities
- Deploy and test robot systems in simulation environments
- Understand best practices for robotics software development

### Certificate Requirements
To earn your certificate for the intermediate path, you must:
- Complete all weekly modules (pass rate: 75% or higher)
- Submit all advanced hands-on projects
- Participate in at least 10 discussion forum posts
- Complete the final comprehensive project (pass rate: 80% or higher)
- Demonstrate proficiency in one specialization track

## Continuing Your Journey

After completing the intermediate path, you'll have strong practical skills in humanoid robotics development. You can then:
- Advance to the expert path for research-level topics
- Specialize in a particular area (perception, control, interaction)
- Contribute to open-source robotics projects
- Pursue independent research projects

### Recommended Next Steps
1. Take our advanced assessment to determine readiness for expert-level content
2. Join our research-oriented community channels
3. Participate in robotics competitions and challenges
4. Begin a significant independent robotics project

The intermediate path prepares you for advanced robotics work and research. Continue building on these foundations as you advance in your robotics journey!
