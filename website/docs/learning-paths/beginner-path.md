## Beginner Learning Path: Foundations of Humanoid Robotics

Welcome to the beginner learning path! This path is designed for students, hobbyists, or professionals who are new to robotics. The curriculum starts with fundamental concepts and gradually builds up to more complex topics.

### Prerequisites
- Basic understanding of programming concepts (any language)
- High school level mathematics
- Curiosity about robotics and AI

### Expected Duration
16-20 weeks (3-4 hours per week)

### Learning Outcomes
By completing this path, you will:
- Understand fundamental robotics concepts and terminology
- Implement simple robot behaviors in simulation
- Navigate basic simulation environments
- Appreciate the challenges and opportunities in humanoid robotics
- Build confidence to pursue more advanced robotics topics

## Week-by-Week Breakdown

### Weeks 1-2: Introduction to Physical AI
**Duration**: 2 weeks | **Time Commitment**: 3-4 hours per week

#### Learning Objectives
- Understand the difference between digital AI and physical AI
- Learn about embodied intelligence and its importance in robotics
- Explore the physical laws that govern robot behavior
- Get familiar with basic robotics terminology

#### Modules
1. **Week 1**: Introduction to Physical AI and Embodied Intelligence
   - What is Physical AI?
   - Digital vs. Physical AI
   - Embodied intelligence concepts
   - Basic kinematics and dynamics

2. **Week 2**: Physical AI and Embodied Intelligence Continued
   - Robot perception basics
   - Physical constraints in robotics
   - Introduction to robot simulation
   - Hands-on: Basic robot simulation exercise

#### Assessment
- Quiz on Physical AI concepts
- Basic simulation exercise completion

- [Physical AI Primer](../week-01-physical-ai/introduction)

3. **Week 5**: ROS 2 Communication Patterns
   - Topics vs. services vs. actions
   - Parameter management

## Continuing Your Journey

After completing the beginner path, you'll have a solid foundation in robotics concepts. You can then:
- Continue to the intermediate path for more advanced topics
- Focus on specific areas of interest (perception, control, interaction)
- Join our community projects and collaborations
- Pursue independent study in specialized robotics areas

### Recommended Next Steps
1. Take our intermediate assessment to determine readiness for next level
2. Join our robotics community Discord channel
3. Participate in monthly beginner meetups
4. Start a personal robotics project using your new skills

Remember, robotics is a journey, not a destination. Enjoy the learning process and don't hesitate to ask questions along the way!
